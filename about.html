Our Heritage

The Order of Preachers

Founded by St. Dominic de Guzmán in 1216, the Order of Preachers — the Dominicans — has been at the forefront of the Church's intellectual and spiritual life for over eight centuries. From St. Thomas Aquinas to St. Catherine of Siena, Dominicans have shaped Catholic theology, philosophy, and spirituality.


The Dominican motto, Veritas (Truth), expresses the heart of our mission: to seek, contemplate, and proclaim the truth of Jesus Christ for the salvation of souls.

Our Home

St. Patrick's Priory, Columbus

St. Patrick's Priory is home to the Dominican community serving St. Patrick Church in the heart of downtown Columbus, Ohio. The friars live a common life of prayer, study, community, and preaching — the four pillars of Dominican life.


The priory is a place where the friars are formed and sustained for their apostolate. Common prayer in the Liturgy of the Hours, shared meals, fraternal recreation, and dedicated study all contribute to the life of this community.

Dominican Life

The Four Pillars

Dominic called this fourfold pattern of life "holy preaching."

✠

Prayer

The Liturgy of the Hours, the Holy Sacrifice of the Mass, and contemplative prayer form the foundation of Dominican life.

☩

Study

Rigorous theological and philosophical study, especially the works of St. Thomas Aquinas, equips the friars to preach with depth.

☧

Community

Fraternal life, shared meals, common recreation, and mutual support sustain the friars in their mission.

✝

Preaching

The fruit of prayer and study is handed on through preaching — the defining charism of the Order of Preachers.
